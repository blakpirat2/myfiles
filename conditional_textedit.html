Late August, first year between the 3-peats:

summoned to the kitchen so I sauntered in meekly.

My brother’s seventeen and in couple more weeks he

is going off to college--I’mma take his room and TV.

I’m eight years old and what’s about to unfold

is a fission in the family vision that my father holds.

He tossed my brother’s journal on the kitchen island,

looked at me and then my mom started crying.

Love is conditional.


I love when love is conditional.

I guess that love is conditional.

I love when love is conditional.


The four of us each a side of this rectangle

The horror of "dad chooses argument’s best angle"

The moral crux - Baptist religion and blessed angels

The oral stuff my bro would not remove from the table.

Behind me out the window’s elevated CTA

while inside we went around in circles like a model train--

My brother saying he wouldn’t rule about being gay

My father turns to me pleading to agree that he can change.

Love’s conditional.


I love when love is conditional.

I’m thinking love is conditional.

I love when love is conditional.


Dropped him off at college, cut financial support.

Not much love until engaged and to a woman, of course.

No matter misery or end in divorce--just happiness

the oldest son had seemed to straighten his course.

Re-married, had my nephew, but I’m still stuck in August

Seeing shadows cast by trains passing and sparks coming off it.

My next few years: the hardest, marked the start of darkness

family thought it was because I was an artist. I learned

Love is conditional.
