story about

He tried “hello” first--

citing mutual friends.

Polite chatter, she expected the usual end--

for him to make this conversation an excuse to go in.

He didn’t, kept listening, she’s getting used to him then…

This is a story about a boy and his beautiful friend.

About phone calls with her mom in the background chiming in

about expectations and givens

assumptions and walks

about mistakes and alcoholism

and Facebook and talks.

About him listening too closely to things she had to say and taking heed--

naively thinking people say what they mean.

It’s about glances and legal adults in parks past midnight

about the chances he had over time but the parts didn’t fit right.

About a spark not light right

a heart told “sit tight”.

Blushing brown cheeks when both asked

“you hit or at least had a kiss, right?”

And one big fight. About being drunk at a party.

About it being hard to see her with him and he’s sorry.

                                [should we hug or just..] walk on?

                                                                        I can’t be your friend.

It’s about thirteen months passed--one of the coldest winters yet

but there’s a warm fire somewhere--

he seen smoke on the internet.

She hit him out the blue,

a scene all-too-innocent.

There was an awkward walk

they stopped twice

while he tried to light his cigarette.

There’s an apology probably pretty moldy in her inbox.

She’s like “man, honestly you told me to go kick rocks.”

Acceptance of blame

admission of guilt

reminder of hurt.

Enough pain.

Friendship rebuilt.

Wonder how it’ll work.

A girl confessing a break-up

and a boy saying I know.

An obligatory “don’t get ideas in your head though”--

so

In an effort to respect the line

he figured it was about time

to get this chick up out his mind...

So on an overcast weekday

filled paint trays with pejoratives

and rolled her face away

with ‘man-eater’

‘groupie’

and of course ‘bitch’.

There was a “fuck it”

that meant something

as long as the echo lingered

plus the nervous tic of time

telling him where to point the finger--

At the past                 (he was fat and insecure)

At the present                 (time is passing and he’s growing more unsure)

And at the future        (there’s a river. there’s a lake. there’s an ocean.)

And those words could only swim so far

so

so far they’re choking and they

drown

in a bottle at the bottom of the sea.

He looks

down

a hollow reflection is all that he can see.
