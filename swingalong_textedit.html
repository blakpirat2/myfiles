I.

My father called his father a nigga. Said he'd urn him. 

I couldn't agree more. Preserve him.

Insurance policy pickup. Deposit put down.

Make the curve in the 'S' in our signature go round.


Swing along my family tree.

Never met uncle Alfonzio; he branched off to PCP.

Broke the thick front door glass--"Tom, I need some more cash".

Gun in hand, my dad said "leave". When he didn't, he squeezed.

Didn't fire but that desire was aimed right at his heart.

Run for cover, my mother's lover would have blasted his brother apart.


My brother's eleven in the next room wishing I would be quiet, but

I was terrible two laughing cause adults fighting is a riot,

like "Al, I was going to kill you, now listen you gotta go".

And I imagine what it's like when the smoke monster recedes in the night.

The two of them in a position that I'll never know.

Protecting my kids and my wife, willing to take my little brother's life.



II.

I see Junebug on Chicago from my girlfriend's car.

He's with some crackhead chick and they're going up or coming down.

The only uncle I know. Banned from neighborhood bars.

Sent south to get clean. But bad news, he's back around.


I see my uncle on Milwaukee while I'm picking up Plan B.

He's way too in my business. Crusty-lipped and jaundiced.

He wants us to go to the park so he can rap with me.

Says he's the last of the Mohicans. He can tell me about all this.


So when I seen him on Damen, I wasn't saying much, I turned.

My mom and I never let him in the house. Now it makes more sense.

I know the look and smell of 'drunk as shit'. It's something I've learned.

And every twist of that doorknob unlocks an event.


So when I see him on Madison, dad in a funeral home,

He sings off-key, bear hugs me, tells me to have babies.

He lost him mom half his life ago, now dad, he's alone.

I try not to scoff at the birthday present of women's socks he gave me.


III.

Intense labor. Long. Etta Mae, pushing out her first. Pain.

Only Slaughter daughter, oxygen deprived brain.

Cursed. Had stunted growth. Back in a hunch, sloped.

I'm eight, answering phones. Can't understand her jumbled quotes.


They decided high school not required. Who'd hire this invalid? 

Some guys still admired, interested.

Eyeing the disability check, deposited monthly.

Finally feeling, anything instead of ugly.


Can't count so self-esteem means a hefty amount.

Never had any trouble reading. So overall, she's beaming.

And since ovary secretions still function semen seeped in.

But being how it was then, no way, no baby, no husband.


They told my mom to raise that baby boy alongside of me.

My mom declined and he became the custody of Illinois.

My aunt's in a low-functioning home somewhere in Skokie.

I would imagine lonely, maybe her son could fill a void, if only.
